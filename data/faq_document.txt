PeopleFlow HR Platform — Customer Support FAQ

About PeopleFlow

PeopleFlow is a cloud-based human resources platform that helps small and mid-sized companies manage their people operations in one place. The current release is PeopleFlow v4.2. The platform covers employee records, time off, payroll coordination, onboarding, performance reviews, and reporting. This FAQ answers the questions our support team receives most often from administrators and employees. If you cannot find an answer here, contact support@peopleflow.example or open a ticket from the Help menu inside the app.

Accounts, Roles, and Permissions

PeopleFlow uses role-based access control with four standard roles: Employee, Manager, HR Admin, and Owner. Employees can view their own profile, request time off, and view their pay stubs. Managers can additionally approve requests and view the records of their direct reports. HR Admins can manage all employee records, run payroll, and configure company policies. The Owner role has full control, including billing and the ability to delete the workspace. Only an Owner can transfer ownership to another user, and a workspace must always have exactly one Owner.

To add a new user, an HR Admin or Owner goes to Settings, then People, then clicks Invite. The invited person receives an email with a sign-up link that expires after 7 days. If the link expires, an admin can resend the invitation from the same screen. Deactivating a user preserves their historical records but immediately revokes their access.

Single Sign-On and Security

PeopleFlow supports Single Sign-On (SSO) through SAML 2.0 and integrates with Okta, Microsoft Entra ID, and Google Workspace. SSO is available on the Business and Enterprise plans. To enable it, an Owner uploads the identity provider metadata under Settings, then Security, then SSO. When SSO is enforced, password login is disabled for all members except the Owner, who keeps a break-glass password in case the identity provider is unavailable.

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is strongly recommended and can be required for all users by an Owner. PeopleFlow supports authenticator apps (TOTP) and hardware security keys. The platform is SOC 2 Type II certified and is GDPR compliant. All data is encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher and encrypted at rest using AES-256. Customer data is hosted in regional data centers; Enterprise customers can choose between United States and European Union data residency.

Time Off and Leave Policies

Full-time employees accrue 15 paid vacation days during their first year of employment. Accrual increases to 20 days after three years of service and to 25 days after seven years. Vacation accrues monthly, so a first-year employee earns 1.25 days per month. Unused vacation up to a maximum of 5 days can be carried into the next calendar year; any balance above that cap is forfeited on December 31.

Employees also receive 10 paid sick days per year, which do not carry over. Parental leave is 12 weeks of paid leave for the primary caregiver and 4 weeks for a secondary caregiver, available after 6 months of employment. To request time off, an employee opens the Time Off tab, selects the dates, chooses the leave type, and submits. The request is routed to their manager for approval. Managers receive an email and an in-app notification, and most requests are approved within two business days. Employees can cancel a pending request at any time, but an approved request must be canceled by a manager.

Onboarding New Hires

The onboarding workflow guides a new hire through their first days. When an HR Admin marks a candidate as hired, PeopleFlow creates an onboarding checklist that can include collecting tax forms, signing the employee handbook, setting up direct deposit, and completing equipment requests. Admins can customize checklist templates under Settings, then Onboarding. Each task can have an owner and a due date, and the new hire sees only the tasks assigned to them. A typical onboarding checklist takes about 30 minutes for the new hire to complete and should be finished before their first payroll cycle.

Payroll Coordination

PeopleFlow does not process payments directly; instead it coordinates payroll by syncing approved hours, time off, and compensation changes to your connected payroll provider. Supported providers include Gusto, ADP, and Paychex. Payroll runs on a configurable schedule that defaults to semi-monthly, with pay dates on the 15th and the last day of each month. HR Admins must approve the payroll summary at least two business days before the pay date so that changes can be transmitted on time. If a pay date falls on a weekend or a public holiday, payroll is processed on the preceding business day.

Pay stubs become available in the Pay tab on the morning of each pay date. Employees can download a PDF of any pay stub and update their direct deposit details, which take effect on the next payroll cycle. Compensation changes such as raises require HR Admin approval and are reflected in the next full pay period.

Time Tracking and Approvals

Hourly employees track time using the Time Tracking feature, which supports a web timer, manual entry, and mobile clock-in. Timesheets are submitted weekly and routed to the employee's manager for approval. Managers can edit entries before approving, and every edit is recorded in an audit log. Overtime is calculated automatically for hours worked beyond 40 in a week, at 1.5 times the regular rate, in accordance with the company's configured overtime policy.

Performance Reviews and Reporting

PeopleFlow includes a performance review module that supports quarterly and annual review cycles, self-assessments, manager reviews, and 360-degree feedback. HR Admins launch a review cycle, select participants, and set a deadline. The Reporting section provides standard reports on headcount, turnover, time-off balances, and compensation, and any report can be exported to CSV. Enterprise customers can build custom reports and schedule them to be emailed automatically.

Plans, Billing, and Support

PeopleFlow offers three plans. The Starter plan costs $6 per seat per month and includes core records and time off. The Business plan costs $12 per seat per month and adds SSO, performance reviews, and integrations. The Enterprise plan offers custom pricing and adds data residency choices, custom reports, and a dedicated success manager. Billing is monthly or annual, and annual billing includes a 15 percent discount. Only the Owner can change the plan or update payment details, under Settings, then Billing.

The Business plan includes a 99.9 percent uptime service level agreement (SLA), and the Enterprise plan includes a 99.95 percent uptime SLA with priority support. Standard support responds to tickets within one business day, while priority support targets a four-hour response during business hours. To reach support, open the Help menu and select Contact Support, or email support@peopleflow.example. Status and incident history are published at status.peopleflow.example.

Data Export and Account Closure

Customers own their data and can export it at any time. An Owner or HR Admin can request a full data export under Settings, then Data, which produces a downloadable archive of employee records and documents in CSV and PDF formats. When a workspace is closed, PeopleFlow retains the data for 30 days to allow recovery, after which it is permanently deleted. Customers can request immediate deletion in writing to comply with data protection requirements.
